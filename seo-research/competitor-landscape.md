# Competitor Landscape — Crescent Health Partners
**Target Markets:** Sumter, SC (primary) + Columbia, SC (secondary)
**Research Date:** 2026-05-29
**Methodology:** Yahoo SERP fetches for wound care, DOT physical, drug testing, primary care, and occupational medicine queries in Sumter SC. NPI registry cross-reference. Direct competitor site analysis. No Semrush MCP available — all ranking signals are based on SERP position observation.

---

## Honest Baseline: Crescent Health Partners Has Zero Competitive Presence

crescenthealthpartners.com does not appear in any SERP checked. It has no indexed pages (only default WordPress content), no GBP, no citations, and no backlinks. This document maps who Crescent is competing against, not where Crescent currently stands.

**The key structural insight:** Wound care is significantly underserved by independent private practices in Sumter. The market is dominated by one hospital system (Prisma Health) and one mobile provider. An independent wound care clinic with a proper web presence can own this space.

## CATEGORY 1: Wound Care Competitors

### Competitor 1: Prisma Health Wound Healing Center — Sumter

- **URL:** prismahealth.org/locations/ambulatory-labs-other/wound-healing-and-hyperbaric-medicine-center-sumter
- **Address:** 100 N. Sumter St., Suite 300, Sumter, SC 29150
- **Phone:** (803) 774-8715
- **Type:** Hospital-based outpatient wound care center (Healogics-managed, Prisma Health-affiliated)
- **Services:** Acute, chronic, and complex wound care; hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT); tissue oxygenation; vascular studies; nutritional assessment; skin grafting
- **Hours:** Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM–5:00 PM (no weekends)
- **Providers:** Kelly Desmond, Henry Moses, Gene Dickerson, Robert Jones, Boan Robyn
- **Domain Rating:** Very High (prismahealth.org is a major health system domain — DR 60+)
- **Organic authority:** Ranks for virtually all Sumter wound care queries
- **GBP:** Yes — confirmed in map pack and SERPs
- **Schema markup:** Likely (large health system, Healogics template)
- **Healthgrades presence:** Yes
- **Key weakness for Crescent:** Hospital-based only. Monday–Friday, 8–5. No evening or weekend appointments. Referred patients only — patients often cannot self-refer. Bureaucratic intake. No mobile or home visit capability. No DOT/occupational health co-location. A private independent clinic with faster access, self-referral, evening hours, and mobile wound care can win the access-sensitive patient segment Prisma cannot serve.
- **How Crescent wins:** Positioning as the accessible, independent alternative. Emphasize self-referral, faster appointments, evening/weekend availability if offered, and the combination of wound care + primary care under one roof.
