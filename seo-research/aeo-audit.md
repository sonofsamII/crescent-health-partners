## 3. Five Highest-Priority AEO Queries for Crescent

### AEO Priority 1: "When should I see a wound care doctor?"

**Why it's #1:** This is the most important patient acquisition question in wound care. The answer directly identifies Crescent as the destination. It drives first-contact intent — the patient doesn't know they need wound care yet.

**What AI engines currently say:** National content (Healogics, WoundCarecenters.org, general medical publishers). No Sumter-specific answer exists.

**How Crescent wins it:** Publish "When Should You See a Wound Care Specialist? [Sumter, SC Guide]" — structure it as a direct question with a clear answer in the first paragraph. Include specific clinical triggers (wound not healing in 2 weeks, increasing size, signs of infection, diabetic foot wound). End with a direct call to action: call Crescent's number or book an appointment.

**The exact trigger phrases AI engines look for:** "A wound that has not started to heal within two weeks" and "any wound on a diabetic foot" — use both.

**Target timeline:** Index within 1–2 weeks of publishing. AI citation potential within 4–8 weeks.

---

### AEO Priority 2: "Is wound care covered by Medicare?"

**Why it's #2:** This is the highest-volume informational wound care query nationally (5,000–15,000 MSV). Medicare recipients are the primary wound care patient population. AI engines answer this frequently. A Crescent page that gives the correct, specific answer — with Crescent positioned as a Medicare-accepting provider — becomes the local trusted answer.

**What AI engines currently say:** Healthline, MedicareFAQ, Medicare.gov. All national. None mention Sumter providers.

**How Crescent wins it:** "Does Medicare Cover Wound Care? What Sumter, SC Patients Need to Know" — explain Part A vs Part B coverage, what services are covered, what the patient pays, and confirm that Crescent accepts Medicare. Include a clear sentence: "Crescent Health Partners in Sumter, SC accepts Medicare for wound care services."

**Medical content note:** Do not promise specific reimbursement amounts. State that Medicare typically covers wound care services and that coverage varies — recommend patients call Medicare or Crescent's billing team to verify their specific plan.

**Target timeline:** 4–8 weeks to AI citation from indexing.

### Signal Group 7: Conversational Content Pattern (Impact: HIGH)

Medical content for AI citation must follow a strict pattern that mirrors how clinicians answer patient questions.

**Patterns that get cited in healthcare:**
- Direct question as H2 → first sentence gives the complete answer
- Specific clinical thresholds: "a wound that has not started healing within two weeks warrants specialist evaluation"
- Named conditions ("diabetic foot ulcer," "venous stasis ulcer") — not generic terms ("leg sore")
- Named procedures in plain language with clinical term in parentheses: "wound cleaning (debridement)"
- Explicit mentions of insurance acceptance: "we accept Medicare, BCBS, and most major insurers"
- Specific geography: "in Sumter, SC" appears multiple times per page
- Provider credentials: "our wound care nurse practitioner Lisa Rivera, MSN, FNP-C, WCC"

**Patterns that do NOT get cited in medical contexts:**
- "Consult your doctor before..." hedges without also providing the actual answer
- Keyword-stuffed paragraphs with no clinical specificity
- Generic descriptions that could describe any clinic anywhere
- Content that lists services without explaining what they involve
